Why Exmoor?

Exmoor, a nature lover's paradise

Every year, over 2 million visitors are drawn to Exmoor for it's starry night skies, wildlife, rolling green landscapes and dramatic coastlines. It is a landscape that has inspired poets, writers and artists for hundreds of years and continues to inspire people today.

This stunning region in the south-west of England captures the hearts of any who visit here. The varied landscape has something for everyone whether it's walking, wildlife watching, or seeking out the popular Exmoor attractions. With so much on offer why would you not visit.

ATTRACTIONS

Unmissable experiences on Exmoor

Dunster Castle

With panoramic views over the Bristol Channel and surrounding countryside, this medieval country home is steeped in history and has plenty to keep you entertained. You can get behind the scenes tours round the castle, visit the watermill or crypt and go exploring around the extensive gardens.

Address:

Dunster Castle, Dunster, Minehead, Somerset, TA24 6NY

What I like about it

There's so much to see and do for all ages both inside the castle and in the grounds that you can easily spend a whole day here.

Learn more

Valley of the Rocks

The Valleys of the Rocks, located a small distance from the village on Lynton, is famous for its herd of goats, its geology an its spectacular views. It is ideal for people wanting to see stunning coastal views and adrenaline-seekers who want to can summit the highest rocks in the area.

Address:

South West Coast Path, Lynton EX35 6JH

What I like about it

The views are breathtaking along the coastline. There are also a variety of walking routes varying in difficulty and length.

Learn more

West Somerset Railway

With 20 miles of railway line along the Bristol Channel Coast and through the Quantock Hills, the West Somerset Railway is the longest heritage route in Britain. There are ten stations along the route, all of which are worth stopping off at.


Address:

The Railway Station, Warren Road, Minehead, Somerset, TA24 5BG

What I like about it

It's a wonderful and picturesque railway line. You can hop on and off the train and explore some of the lovely locations enroute.
